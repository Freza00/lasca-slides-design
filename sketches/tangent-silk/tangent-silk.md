# Tangent Silk

*An algorithmic movement for curves that are not drawn, but implied.*

---

## Philosophy

**Tangent Silk** is the study of the *envelope* — the smooth curve that emerges, uninvited, from a family of strictly straight lines. Draw a line. Draw another. Draw three hundred more, each one rigid, each one obedient to a pair of generator curves and a parameter sweep. At no point does the algorithm draw a curve. And yet, the eye insists — it sees arcs, petals, bells, horns, the calligraphic stroke of a quill that was never there. The curve is the ghost of the lines. This is the movement's central koan: *the line is the author; the curve is the reader*.

The mathematics is austere to the point of severity. Two parameterized paths, A(t) and B(t), each traced through space. A mapping function m(t) that decides which point on A pairs with which point on B — linear, reversed, exponentially warped, sinusoidally shuffled. For each t in a dense sampling of [0, 1], draw the straight segment from A(t) to B(m(t)). Nothing more. The *envelope* — the visible curve — is the locus of tangency of this line family. It is not computed. It is not stored. It simply appears, because geometry is honest. This is the product of deep mathematical expertise pretending, with great discipline, to be effortless.

The aesthetic is what separates an equation from a cover. A master of computational aesthetics knows that the pinch matters as much as the spread — the source curve must be short enough to suggest a point of origin, a moment of decision from which everything else fans outward; the target curve must be wide enough to let the envelope breathe. The mapping function is where soul enters math: a linear pairing yields a rigid fan, a reversed one yields a parabolic swoop, and a carefully warped exponent yields the particular lyric quality of *institutional gravity* — the quiet authority of a report cover that expects to be read. Every parameter has been refined across countless iterations by someone at the top of their field.

Restraint is the animating virtue. Tangent Silk refuses noise. Each line is drawn thin, rendered in a single color that eases gradually along its length — a whisper of gradient, not a shout. The field behind is pure, often paper-white, sometimes deep navy; either way, it is empty space treated with respect. Where lines cross, they do not collide — they *interfere*, and the interference is itself a quiet music, a beat pattern visible only if you stop to listen. The painstakingly calibrated stroke weight and alpha are what separate a masterwork from a physics homework.

Dual fans, when present, share a pinch point and spray in opposing directions — the classic butterfly, the tilted hourglass, the ribbon tied and pulled through. This is where the philosophy reveals its rigor: the two fans are not copies but mirrors, each running the same algorithm with reversed polarity, their shared source creating a *crease* that the eye reads as structural meaning. Meaning from symmetry. The algorithm does not symbolize — it declares.

The final piece should feel unarguably *designed*. Not contrived. Not maximal. Not clever. Simply inevitable — as if a jeweler, a draftsman, and a mathematician had together decided that this, and only this, was the correct configuration of the pencil of lines. When a viewer reads the work as *composition* rather than *computation*, the algorithm has succeeded. The straight lines vanish into the smooth curve they never were. The silk remains.

## Conceptual Seed

The aesthetic points at the research-brief cover — those spare, geometrical compositions that advisory firms place on quarterly reports to signal that the contents have been thought about carefully by expensive people. The reference is embedded in what the work *chooses not to do*: no riot of color, no crowding, no emotional gesture. Only restraint, tangency, and the willingness to let mathematics carry the whole weight of the design. A sibling of *Meridian Silk*, but where Meridian Silk weaves with sine, Tangent Silk weaves with tangency itself.
